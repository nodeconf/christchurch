NodeConf Christchuch - Confirmed Speakers

Justin Searls

Talk: The social coding contract

Justin Searls has two professional passions: writing great software and sharing what he’s learned to help others write even greater software. He helped start a software agency called Test Double, whose crack team of double agents solve complex problems with clean and simple solutions.

Twitter: @searls
Github: searls

Aria Stewart

Talk: How to read code

Aria Stewart is a tentacle wrangler with the Kraken.js team at PayPal, working on the open source web framework and trying to keep a multi-armed monster of requirements and interests from wreaking havoc on applications.

Twitter: @aredridel
Github: aredridel

Rod Vagg

Talk: The future of node

Rod Vagg is Director of Engineering with NodeSource. Rod is known for his work across the Node.js ecosystem, including in the Node.js databases community and for the creation of key NodeSchool workshoppers. He is passionate about Node.js and its future as a wide-spread, general-purpose and enterprise programming platform. This passion has lead to his involvement in io.js as a way to reinvigorate the core of Node.js and bring it in alignment with the future of JavaScript.

Twitter: @rvagg
Github: rvagg

James “substack” Halliday

Talk: Webapps of the future

open source librarian, unix philosopher, tiny module author

Website: substack.net
Twitter: @substack
Github: substack

Tim Oxley

Talk: Practical ES6

Tim Oxley is the founder and organiser of the CampJS conference in Australia, Singapore NodeSchool events and the SingaporeJS meetup group. Tim is a NodeUp co-host and a developer with NodeSource.

Twitter: @secoif
Github: timoxley

Dominic Tarr

Talk: A cryptographically secure decentralised todo list

Dominic has spent several years studying databases and distributed systems, and is now applying that learning towards cryptographically secure, replication-first databases

Website: dominictarr.com
Twitter: @dominictarr
Github: dominictarr

Sarah Hui

Talk: Straight up nodebots

Sarah is lead front end engineer at Vend’s HQ in Auckland. She’s been part of the rocketship since way back, building their famed point of sale, bringing together their rapidly expanding engineering & product team, and admiring moustaches. As a lover of JavaScript and beautiful things, she spreads the good word of technology & development, has been known to tinker with nodebots, and has recently got a bluetooth speaker that has truly impacted her life. Code life, thug life.

Twitter: @sehsarah
Github: sehsarah
